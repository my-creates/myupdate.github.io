Here is my Artist Statement

This is what makes me who I am and it's been this way forever

My artwork embodies an overactive imagination, one that never stops and reason my ideas continue to spew out like a never ending rainbow. Digital tools at my hand able to create whatever I want to convey. It's strange though. Sometimes it shows the happy wonderland my imagination really and sometimes some pretty dark undertones make its way into the artwork. When gazing upon my artwork sometimes, a lot has expressed that they feel a happy sensation with a burning sense of paranoia. That's what I want you to feel.

I want you to feel like what I have just drawn is too happy or something is a little off here. Which there is. A lot of my work, in the recent months, is meant to spark some trust issues. It’s meant to make you overthink things, to have the same anxiety I have for my daily life and for my art. It shows just how dark something so happy can be. Such as a spider, it can be scary but I have made it somewhat cute in a way. Just a sweet thing that could potentially destroy someone with just a small amount of venom. It is inspired by how cutesy I can make a particular thing, it could be as simple as a car or a tree. It can also be inspired by music or other art that I’ve seen on social media. It inspires a series of new ideas that causes work to never really gets done. Then I go on a tangent and write down multiple ideas that lead to a sketch or just straight drawing that would take forever because I will get frustrated with it or think that it could be better. Then I’d remember that I am my worst critic and go back to what I was working on before. But will it ever get done, probably not.

I tend to switch projects a lot due to my ideas bouncing off of each other creating new things and new wills. Thus, it seems, nothing will ever get done. The ones I do finish, turn out to be more in my specific comfort zone. The cute ones with the suspicion of darkness lurking beneath those happy colors. It is a complete recognition of what my imagination actually is. A cute little wonderland that’s open all day with the presence of darkness slowly threatening the very likelihood of the cute establishment.

That's what my art plays off of. It goes off of something that has been turned undoubtedly cute even though it still keeps its own creepiness. It is brought on by multiple inspirations but the main source would have to be my imagination plays a big part in my artistic process. It always seems active and at full power when there’s music involved though. It makes one wonder what is truly going on in my head and why does it sanction such a cute representation? Well, that’s just the way my art works, playing off of an overactive imagination that has no restraints on how far it could go and drawing is a perfect outlet for it.
